# Interstice — Positioning & Messaging

## One-Liner

**Interstice: Voice-to-action AI orchestration for your wrist.**

## Elevator Pitch (30 seconds)

You speak a command into your wearable. Your AI CEO hears it, breaks it into tasks, and delegates to a team of specialist agents — research, communications, development, even a call agent that dials real phones. They execute in parallel, share data with each other in real-time, and your CEO reports back through your wrist. It's not a chatbot. It's a company that runs on your voice.

## Target Persona

**Primary: The Overloaded Solopreneur**
- Running a business alone or with 1-2 people
- Drowning in multi-step tasks: research a market, draft an outreach email, build a landing page, follow up with a lead
- Uses 5+ tools daily (ChatGPT, email, Notion, Figma, phone)
- Wants to delegate but can't afford to hire
- Already wearing smart devices, comfortable with voice

**Pain Points:**
1. "I spend 3 hours on a task that should take 20 minutes because it has 5 steps across 5 tools"
2. "ChatGPT can research OR write, but can't do both and connect them"
3. "I need an outreach email that references actual market data, not generic fluff"
4. "I want to delegate but I'm the only person here"

## Positioning Statement

For solopreneurs and small teams who need to execute complex, multi-step business operations, **Interstice** is an AI orchestration system that turns a single voice command into coordinated action across a team of specialist agents. Unlike chatbots that handle one task at a time, Interstice decomposes your intent into parallel workstreams, agents share data with each other in real-time, and approval gates keep you in control of actions that matter.

## Competitive Positioning

**vs. ChatGPT / Claude (single agent):** They're one agent doing one thing. We're five agents doing five things simultaneously, sharing data in real-time.

**vs. CrewAI / AutoGen / LangGraph (multi-agent frameworks):** They're developer tools for building agent pipelines in code. We're a finished product you speak into. Also: none of them connect to hardware, none make real phone calls, none have approval gates.

**vs. Humane AI Pin / Rabbit R1 (AI wearables):** They failed because one agent isn't enough. We use the same hardware approach (wearable, voice-first) but with an org chart of specialists instead of a single assistant. Also, we built on OMI which is $89 and open-source, not $700 proprietary hardware.

**vs. Paperclip AI (agent orchestration):** Paperclip is our architectural inspiration — we use their heartbeat model and org chart delegation. But Paperclip has no voice input, no wearable integration, no real-time inter-agent communication, and no action execution (calls, emails). We took the best parts and made them voice-native.

## The "Interstice" Name

An **interstice** is a small gap between things. The gap between what you want done and it actually getting done. The gap between your intent and execution. Interstice lives in that gap — it's the AI that turns "I need this" into "it's done."
