# Interstice — Brand Guide

## Brand Identity

### Name
**Interstice** (noun): A small gap between things. Specifically, the gap between what you want done and it getting done.

Pronunciation: in-TER-stiss

### Brand Personality
- **Precise, not flashy.** Like Anthropic — we let the technology speak. No gratuitous animations, no hype copy.
- **Confident, not arrogant.** Like Perplexity — we know what we built is good. We show it, not tell it.
- **Technical, not intimidating.** We explain complex architecture in plain language.
- **Minimal, not empty.** Every element earns its place. If it doesn't serve the user, it doesn't exist.

### Voice & Tone
- **Direct.** Short sentences. Active voice. No hedge words ("might", "could potentially", "we believe").
- **Specific.** Numbers over adjectives. "5 agents in parallel" not "powerful multi-agent system."
- **Grounded.** Every claim maps to real product capability. If we can't demo it, we don't say it.
- **Human.** We're building AI that works for real people. No "synergy", no "paradigm shift", no "revolutionary."

### Words We Use
- orchestration (not automation)
- delegate (not assign)
- specialists (not bots)
- approval gate (not safety check)
- findings (not data transfer)
- voice-first (not voice-enabled)
- inter-agent communication (not data pipeline)

### Words We Don't Use
- synergy
- revolutionary / disruptive
- AI-powered (everything is AI-powered, it means nothing)
- cutting-edge / state-of-the-art
- seamless / frictionless
- unlock / unleash / supercharge
- game-changing

## Visual Direction

### Mood
Think: Anthropic's documentation meets Linear's dashboard. Clean, functional, information-dense without being cluttered.

### Color Approach
- **Dark mode primary** — matches the dashboard aesthetic already built
- **Accent color for agent status** — agents glow when active, dim when idle
- **Minimal color palette** — 2-3 colors max. Let the real-time data be the visual interest.

### Typography Direction
- **Monospace for technical elements** — code, agent output, task IDs
- **Clean sans-serif for everything else** — Inter, or similar
- **No decorative fonts.** The brand is the architecture, not the typography.

### Imagery
- **Screenshots of the real dashboard** — not mockups, not illustrations
- **Architecture diagrams** — clean, minimal, showing actual data flow
- **The OMI device on a wrist** — physical artifact grounds the abstract AI concept

## Messaging Hierarchy

### Level 1: What Is It (5 seconds)
> Voice-to-action AI orchestration for your wrist.

### Level 2: How Does It Work (15 seconds)
> Speak a command. Your AI CEO decomposes it, delegates to specialist agents, they execute in parallel sharing data with each other, and your CEO reports back through your wrist.

### Level 3: Why Is It Different (30 seconds)
> Unlike chatbots that handle one task at a time, Interstice runs a team of five specialist agents simultaneously. Research feeds data to Communications and Developer in real-time. Approval gates keep you in control of external actions. And it all starts with your voice, through an $89 open-source wearable.

### Level 4: Why Does It Matter (60 seconds)
> The AI wearable market is $32B and growing 28% annually. The leaders — Humane and Rabbit — failed because single-agent architectures can't handle complex business tasks. OMI is the fastest-growing open-source wearable with 250+ apps, but zero orchestration layers. Interstice is the first multi-agent system built natively for voice-first hardware, with the approval gates and inter-agent communication that make real-world action execution trustworthy.

## Content Principles

1. **Show, don't tell.** Dashboard screenshot > feature description. Demo > pitch deck.
2. **Specificity is credibility.** "10 Convex tables, 5 agents, 3 skills, 24 hours" > "powerful and comprehensive."
3. **Architecture is the brand.** The org chart, the data flow, the approval gates — these ARE the visual identity.
4. **The demo IS the marketing.** A phone ringing mid-presentation sells better than any copy.
